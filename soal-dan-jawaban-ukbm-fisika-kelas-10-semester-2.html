Subject: English
Material: Pronoun

Explanation:
Subjective pronoun explain the subject of the sentence, consists of I, You, We, They, He, She, It

Objective pronoun explain the object of the sentence, consists of Me, You, Us, Them, Him, Her, It

Possessive adjective explain about belonging to an object, consists of My, Your, Our, Their, His, Her, Its

Possessive pronoun explain about belonging to an object without the lable of the object, consists of Mine, Yours, Ours, Theirs, His, Hers, Its.

Answer:

------------------------------------------------------

1. Emily wrote to her dad and told him about the report
-> "her" dad = Emily's dad
-> "him" = Emily's dad

2. Emily's dad likes her reports and always reads them aloud
-> "her" reports = Emily's reports
-> "them" = reports

3. Emily went to the library and she looked up Alexander Graham Bell
-> "she" = Emily

4. The students asked the librarian to help them
-> "them" = the students

5. The librarian said, "please come to me for any books".
-> "me" = the librarian

6. The librarian was very helpful and Emily said, "Thanks, you have been a great help".
-> "you" = the librarian

7. Emily admired Bell and wrote about him
-> "him" = Bell

8. Bell taught deaf pupils because they wanted to learn to speak
-> "they" = pupils

9. Bell used "Vissible Speech" because it helped the deaf
-> "it" = Vissible Speech

10. Watson and Bell worked together. They carried out experiments with electricity.
-> "they" = Watson and Bell

11. Watson spoke into the telephone, it carried his voice to Bell
-> "it" = telephone

12. Bell had invented the telephone. People congratulated him.
-> "him" = Bell

- Well done!

- Please accept my warmest congratulations

- Congratulations on your …………..
